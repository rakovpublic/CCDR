The code has an ambiguity in what "N" means across the three routes. Let me diagnose precisely:
The AQN route computes k_eff = 1/6, then N = 1/k_eff − 1 = 5. But the question is: 5 what? The DA/O route gives 6 compact internal dimensions. The HaPPY route gives 6 total bulk dimensions. These are counting different things.
The fix depends on the correct physical interpretation of k_eff:
Interpretation A: k_eff = (visible sectors) / (total sectors) = 1/6. Total sectors = 6. If each sector corresponds to one internal dimension, then N_internal = 6, of which 1 is visible (acoustic) and 5 are dark (optical). This gives N(AQN) = 6 total, matching DA/O and HaPPY.
Interpretation B (what the code uses): k_eff = 1/(N+1) where N = number of dark sectors = 5. Then total = 5 dark + 1 visible = 6. But the code reports N = 5 because it's counting only the dark sectors.
The resolution: all three routes give the same number when counted consistently. The total is 6 in all cases. The code's N_AQN = 5 counts only the extra (dark) sectors, while DA/O and HaPPY count the total. The correct comparison is:

DA/O: 6 compact internal dimensions (total internal)
AQN: 6 total sectors (1 visible + 5 dark)
HaPPY: 6 total bulk dimensions

All three give 6. The code has a counting convention mismatch, not a physics inconsistency. You should update the AQN line to N_AQN = int(round(1/k_eff)) (= 6, the total number of sectors including visible) rather than subtracting 1.
This is actually a useful finding — it forces you to be precise about what N means in each context. The published article should state explicitly: "N = 6 is the total number of internal sectors, of which 1 is the visible (acoustic) sector and 5 are the dark (optical) sectors."
